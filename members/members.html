SFHC Compound Setup

Living Conditions
Rooms

Shared with 1-2 roommates. Twin beds with bednets, sheets, and pillows provided. Power outlets available.

Hygiene

Shared shower/toilet/sink. Warm water usually available. Use BOTTLED water for oral hygiene.

Meals

Breakfast/Dinner served in common room. Staples: Meat/eggs, rice/noodles, safe fruits/veg. Coffee/tea safe.

Connectivity

Wifi reaches all areas. Common room used for work and hanging out.

Drinking Water

ABSOLUTE RULE: Drink only bottled drinks. There is no filtered tap water available at the compound.

Laundry Service

Dirty laundry is collected ~once per week, washed for you, and returned the next day.

Red Flag Rules

Non-Negotiable

These guidelines exist to protect your safety, the integrity of our partnership, and the mission of Ekwendeni Hospital and SFHC. Failure to comply may result in immediate dismissal from the program.

01. Transportation
  • NO motorcycles or informal taxis.
  • NO travel without a local chaperone.
  • Stay within supervised routes only.
02. Buddy System
  • NEVER leave the compound alone.
  • Minimum 1 buddy for all excursions.
  • Check-in with program leads via SMS.
03. Substance Use
  • Moderation and respect are required.
  • Never interfere with safety or role.
  • Zero tolerance for illicit substances.
04. Health Reporting
  • Report fever or flu symptoms immediately.
  • Early triage is critical for malaria.
  • Do not "wait it out" under any case.
05. Privacy & Media
  • NO photos of faces without permission.
  • Total compliance with patient laws.
  • When in doubt: do not post online.
06. Gift Giving
  • NO direct gifts to local individuals.
  • Gratitude is handled as a collective.
  • Follow lead clinician's guidance.

Situational Awareness Tips

The Basics
  • Follow Red Flags: No exceptions.
  • Safe Water: Drink BOTTLED only. Use it for brushing teeth too.
  • Passport Safety: Keep real passport at the compound. Carry a printed copy on you.
  • Valuables: Leave laptops/iPads at the compound. No safe storage at the hospital.
Money & Location
  • Body Storage: Use body-hugging fanny packs/bags for phone/cards/cash.
  • Backpacks: Not safe to leave unless they are on your person at all times.
  • Location Sharing: Share location with family and student leaders via smartphone.
  • Offline Maps: Always have the Malawi area downloaded.
By participating, you agree to follow these guidelines in the spirit of humility and responsibility.
